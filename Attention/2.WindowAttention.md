## The problem - the quadratic prison

vanilla self attention (2017) was brilliant but had a major flaw - it sucked when it came to scale. it had a time and memory complexity of $O(N^2)$. each token had to be computed with the attention score of the every other token. for a short para of just 512 tokens the number of calculations were more than 2,00,000. if we pushed the context window it would increase the number of calculation and further blow up the GPU memory. 

## Breakthrough

authors realised that language is intensly local. think about it, you are reading a sentence that is somewhere in the middle of the chapter, do you actually need to know the first word of the chapter? NO. April 2020 - AI2 published "Longformer:The Long-Document Tranformer" and it formalised the "sliding window attention".

&gt; **Aha moment**: "instead of letting the token look at every other token let it just look at its negihbours with the fixed window of w".

this changed the entire complexity scene as now it reduced from $O(N^2)$ to a linear $O(N*w)$

but again there was an immediate trade off in all of this - it reduced compute but created a blind spot as well - what if somehting on page 10 actually was related to something on page1.
the "strict sliding windows" lost the critical "global" context that made transformers so powerful in the first place.

but what about attention sinks? 

the researches gave SWAT framework - enabled models to be trained with sliding window attention rather than full global attention.

### What was new in all of it?

* standard tf use softmax which lead to attention sinks - where the models give very high attention to the initial tokens of the model. this [paper](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2309.17453) also discussed a solution to this problem where : if they just simply removed the initial tokens of the model - i.e removing the attention sinks it lead to removal of a considerable portion of the denominator in the SoftMax function in attention computation. This alteration leads to a significant shift in the distribution of attention scores away from what would be expected in normal inference settings. 

their solution: rolling kvc with attention sinks: keep attention sinks permanently in memory ( do not remove them ), keep the first four tokens fixed and slide over on the rest of the tokens. the paper shows that transformers rely on statistical equilibra formed during training and seemingly meaningless tokens can serve structural mathematical roles.

### What SWAT did differently here?

get rid of softmax - use sigmoid - the problem of attention sinks was due to softmax - it dumped high attention score to the initial tokens as : post softmax all the sum of all distributions should be = 1, so where does this mass go? softmax is biased towards consistently present positions, early tokens are always there and they become a statistical attractor.

by getting sigmoid in the picture we eliminate the competitive nature of Softmax (where all scores must sum to 1), effectively preventing the model from needing an attention sink.

but one problem - each word is now getting its own calculation independently, meaning the model forgets where the word is located in the relative structure of the sentence which leads to training instability. 

**solution - RoPE**: RoPE reintroduces this missing information by explicitly rotating the Query ($Q$) and Key ($K$) vectors according to their position in the sequence. This ensures the model has a "hard-coded" geometric sense of where tokens are, even without Softmax.

### Balanced ALiBi for Density Management

**Whais is AliBi?** read in deep [here](https://medium.com/@pajakamy/alibi-attention-with-linear-biases-942abe042e9f)

for now a short summary: ALiBi (Attention with Linear Biases) is a position encoding method for Transformers that replaces traditional sinusoidal or learned positional embeddings with simple linear biases added to attention scores.

**Core idea**: Instead of encoding position information into the input embeddings, ALiBi adds a penalty to attention scores that increases linearly with distance between tokens. This naturally encodes recency bias—closer tokens attend more strongly than distant ones.

let us say we trained our model on 512 seq of words , but if at the time of testing you gave it a word out of this limit it will crash. so models usually lose coherence when they are asked to process sequences that are longer that what they saw during training. 

to counter this ALiBi was created - to allow "Extrapolation". Instead of adding a "position vector" to the words, ALiBi adds a penalty directly to the Attention Scores ($QK^T$) before the Softmax.

the formulae:

$$
Score = \frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d}} - (m \cdot \text{distance})
$$

where:
* $m$: A fixed "slope" (different for each attention head). 
* Distance: How many tokens apart the two words are

**Example:**
Imagine a small model with one attention head where the slope $m = 1$. The Sentence: "The cat sat on the mat." Let's look at the word "mat" (position 6) looking back at "The" (position 1). 
* Calculate Raw Score: The model calculates how much "mat" relates to "The" using $QK^T$. Let's say the raw score is 10.
* Calculate Distance: The distance between position 6 and position 1 is 5. 
* Apply the ALiBi Penalty: $Penalty = m \times \text{distance}$ $Penalty = 1 \times 5 = 5$ Final Score: $10 - 5 = \mathbf{5}$.

Now, let's look at "mat" (pos 6) looking at "the" (pos 5):
* Raw Score: Assume it's also 10.
* Distance: $6 - 5 = \mathbf{1}$. 
* Penalty: $1 \times 1 = 1$.
* Final Score: $10 - 1 = \mathbf{9}$.

**Result**: Even though the raw relationship was the same (10), ALiBi makes the model focus more on the word 1 step away (Score 9) than the word 5 steps away (Score 5)

in this paper they also use "negative penalty" along with positive penalty - as positive penalties allow you to forget the past as you move on , but the balanced alibi having both of these slopes allows you to focues on the immediate path (recent context) and distant past (long term history). this balance is what allows SWAT to compress massive amounts of information into its window without the memory blowing up.

### RoPE and Training Stability

RoPE explicitly encodes positional information into the hidden states, which provides the model with a much stronger geometric sense of where tokens are in relation to one another.
